Today I didn't really think to drink. Except some idle pangs and thoughts. I ate a lot of food very cheaply, a lot of hot food. There's something about how the brain only really seams to notice change rather than objective quality. When one is used to hot meals, then they are just another thing to enjoy, but changing from hot meals to cold, or the reverse you learn to appreciate them. It's only really in those changes that conscious thought seems to live. The brain as an organ is predisposed to normalcy to reduce its cognitive load. You don't notice the way things are truely because what is there to notice. Your eyes have an easier time finding something moving. Finding something different, until that too gets assimilated into normalcy. 

That's the same too with change. Slow change goes unnoticed because it is so proximate to the norm that there's no particular reason for the brain to notice it. There's nothing out in those woods. They grow, they shrink. You move on, you die. That's that. Forced to live in a world you couldn't possibly notice. 

Most of what's out there is probably boring. There's the things out there you could never see. The rest of the unvierse. Heat, light, air, gravity. Mostly empty. A whole lot of nothing. Nothing worth seeing. Then there's the world, places you will never see. People you will never meet. There's a lot of never out there. If you just go and find it.
