The warnings. It kept on shouting at me. Overspeed. Overspeed. Overspeed. I started to move sluggish. My imagined metal body not floating in the air like it did. I kept on pressing the throttle further. 

I was a missile. I was a shell. I was the canon. I was the propellant. I was the gunpowder. I was the shell casing.

Paved in twisted barrels, dud shells and rocket shrapnel. 

It was just a little further now. It was like being in pursuit of a meal, the way the smell surrounded me now. I was so hungry. Biological imperatives to kill. A beast doesn’t make paths. A beast doesn’t try to right the wrongs left behind. There is only one road open to such a beast. It was above. It was to follow the smell of the prey. The darkened sky. 

The last line of defenses were light. There was little left to do. Drones couldn’t reach back here enough. This defensive fortification had extracted all the resources it could, there was little more to be gained. I reached into the sky. I gripped the cables and tore them apart. Immediate pleasure filling every single nodule in my brain. Ripping. Ripping. Ripping. The sky now shone out all the clearer. I couldn’t feel the sun’s warmth. Not anymore. That didn’t matter. I felt so good all the same. With the drones cut friendly forces could advance, but that left the defenses. The concrete was easy to break, it felt so good. The rubble fell apart. The marks in it were jagged, not made by a human, not made by a machine, they were made by an animal. Desperate and without pattern except the pattern of random motions that marked a predator gorging itself on still warm meat. The core was covered by the collapse. The feeling was intense. A mind that was now so immersed in happy chemicals that rational thought would not be able to continue. Washed away in bliss. The one who conquered the hill standing there idle hands still buried inside the rubble.
