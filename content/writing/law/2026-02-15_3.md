ACTUS REUS CAUSATION
R v. White -> Cyanide in lemonade heart attack, an unnatural act to break the chain of causation
R v. Kennedy -> Free informed decision of a third party breaks the chain, heroin supplier not guilty
R v. Williams/R v. Davies -> Free informed decision of the hitch hiker to jump from the car resulting in their death broke the chain, unnatural
R v. Jordan -> Stab wound death from pneumonia following bad medical treatment, unnatural act broke the chain BUT generally medical acts not the same
R v. Wallace -> Acid face wound did not break the chain even in suicide perhaps an upending of precedent
R v. Cheshire -> Windpipe medical care potential cause of death but rebuffed because the treatment was not negligent
R v. Smith -> Soldier stabbed dropped twice and given the wrong treatment nevertheless still held to not break the chain Significant and operative
R v. Gowans -> Coma put the victim in a vulnerable state to die by the
infection they later died on, so the act was still operant
R v. A -> Parked car on the hard shoulder at 4:30am with no lights on, collision with a lorry which then kills a pedestrian, causation found on appeal.
R v. Field -> Old man whiskey case causation found even with a free and informed action
R v. Mitchell -> Causation found when someone else was the target of an unlawful act but someone else died
Malcherek (1981) -> Brain death
R v. Rafferty -> Operant ie, there is no novus actus intravenous
R v. Blaue -> Omissions do not break the chain
R v. Roberts -> Reasonably foreseeable, V jumping out of a car going at speed in response to indecent suggestions, however it need only be the natural conclusion to be causal. Roberts also not acting freely. 
R v. Hughes -> More than minimal cause
R v. Jordan -> US airforce personnel who were involved in a stabbing, then administered antibiotics that V was known to be allergic to
