title: Gross Negligence Manslaughter
---

The operative case here is:

R v. Adomako -> Anaesthetist who failed to notice for six minutes that a tube that suppied oxygen to a patient had become disconnected from a ventilator, as a result the patient died. D was convicted of Gross Negligence Manslaughter. His appeal was dismissed. It established the 5 elements of Gross Negligence Manslaughter.

1. There must be a duty, following Evans. 

2. There must be a breach of that duty. In adomako the breach was not excercising the position they had with reasonable care and skill as should be expected. Broughton the court may find that the steps taken were inadequate.

3. Causing the death. In Broughton a 90% Chance of saving the victim was held to be insufficient to be a cause, whereaas in Misra 95% was high enough to find that. Almost certain the victim would have lived but for the breach of duty. 

4. Serious and obvious risk of death. Found in R v. Rose the optician case with the 7 year old boy. It is serious and obvious at the time of breach. Kuddus the Indian restaurant nut allergy case follows the same lines.

5. Gross negligence. It must be shown that the breach of duty was gross and not slight. Were the breaches so bad as to require judicial intervention. Found in Adomako.
